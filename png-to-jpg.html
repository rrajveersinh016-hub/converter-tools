Frequently Asked Questions

Will I lose quality converting PNG to JPG?

Because JPG is a lossy compression format, there is technically a slight loss in mathematical quality compared to the original lossless PNG. However, our converter provides a quality slider you can adjust up to 100% to ensure visual fidelity remains virtually identical while still saving file size.

What is the difference between PNG and JPG?

PNGs are lossless image files that support transparent backgrounds, making them perfect for logos or web graphics, but they typically have very large file sizes. JPGs are universally supported, highly compressed files ideal for standard photography where transparent backgrounds are not required.
